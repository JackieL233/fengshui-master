# Wellbeing Adapter

Use this file when the user asks for feng shui guidance on sleep, stress, focus, energy, burnout, mood-adjacent concerns, recovery routines, health-adjacent environments, bedroom comfort, light, air, noise, clutter, or ergonomics.

## Core Rule

Use feng shui as an environmental and symbolic support lens. Do not diagnose, treat, or promise healing. Medical symptoms, mental-health crises, mold, gas, electrical hazards, structural issues, or safety concerns require qualified professional support.

## Wellbeing Intake

Ask for:

- Main concern: sleep, stress, focus, fatigue, noise, clutter, light, air, ergonomics.
- Duration and severity if the user volunteers it.
- Space context: bedroom, desk, shared room, ventilation, dampness, sunlight, noise, temperature.
- Safety red flags: mold, gas smell, electrical issues, structural damage, self-harm risk.
- Current care or professional advice if relevant.
- What changes are practical and safe.

## Feng Shui Translation

| Feng shui concept | Wellbeing translation |
| --- | --- |
| Yin quality | rest, darkness, quiet, privacy, lower stimulation |
| Yang quality | daytime movement, light, air, activation |
| Backing | stable bed/chair, routine, support, safety |
| Sha qi | noise, glare, sharp edges, clutter pressure, conflict, hazard |
| Leakage | poor sleep boundary, screens, drafts, stress spillover |
| Stagnation | dampness, stale air, unused clutter, lack of movement |

## Five-Phase Wellbeing Lens

| Phase | Supportive expression | Excess risk | Adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | movement, growth, morning routine | irritability, overplanning | metal simplification |
| Fire | warmth, joy, daylight | overstimulation, insomnia | water cooling |
| Earth | nourishment, routine, grounding | heaviness, clutter | wood movement |
| Metal | clean air, boundaries, order | rigidity, harshness | fire warmth |
| Water | rest, reflection, cooling | isolation, fear | earth stability |

## Practical Priorities

1. Safety: hazards, exits, wiring, gas, mold, structure.
2. Air and light: ventilation, daylight, glare, humidity.
3. Sleep support: bed backing, quiet, darkness, device boundaries.
4. Ergonomics: chair, desk, monitor, repetitive strain.
5. Symbolic adjustments: color, plant, water, mirror, ritual only after practical issues.

## Output Pattern

1. **Wellbeing reality**: concern, environment, red flags, constraints.
2. **Environmental read**: sleep, air, light, noise, clutter, ergonomics.
3. **Symbolic layer**: yin-yang, phases, sha, leakage, stagnation.
4. **Low-risk adjustments**: safety and reversible changes first.
5. **Boundaries**: no diagnosis; seek qualified care for symptoms or hazards.

## Forbidden Claims

Do not promise healing, diagnose illness, tell users to stop treatment, or attribute disease to layout. If crisis, abuse, severe symptoms, or environmental hazards appear, prioritize immediate qualified support.
