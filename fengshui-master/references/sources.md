# Sources and Research Notes

This file lists source categories and anchor references for maintaining FengShui Master. It is not a substitute for deeper lineage-specific study.

## Source Posture

Use three layers:

1. **Classical anchors**: Chinese classics and traditional terminology that shape the conceptual vocabulary.
2. **Scholarly or reference summaries**: academic, encyclopedia, or museum-level descriptions of feng shui history and schools.
3. **Lineage practice notes**: san he, san yuan, xuan kong, ba zhai, and modern symbolic-bagua rules, labeled by school.

## Classical Anchors

- **I Ching / Yijing / Zhouyi**: background for yin-yang image thinking and the eight trigrams.
- **Shang Shu, Hong Fan chapter**: one early classical source for five phases language.
- **Zang Shu / Book of Burial attributed to Guo Pu**: often cited in feng shui history for the wind-water phrasing and burial-site qi logic.
- **Later luopan and calendrical manuals**: source families for 24 mountains, stems/branches, san he, san yuan, and xuan kong practice.

## Verification Notes

- Verify current claims about xuan kong periods, annual stars, and calendar boundaries before adding them as operational rules.
- Treat popular modern cures as lower-confidence unless tied to a named school or practical design reason.
- Avoid copying long passages from copyrighted translations. Summarize and cite externally in project docs if needed.
