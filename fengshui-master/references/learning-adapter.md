# Learning Adapter

Use this file when the user asks for feng shui or five-phase guidance on studying, exams, learning plans, practice routines, focus, memory, creative learning, research, or educational environments.

## Core Rule

Use feng shui as a symbolic and environmental support lens for attention, rhythm, study flow, and review cycles. Do not replace pedagogy, tutoring, evidence-based study methods, accommodations, or medical/mental-health support.

## Learning Intake

Ask for:

- Subject, level, and deadline.
- Goal: understanding, exam score, certification, portfolio, language, creative skill.
- Current schedule and bottleneck.
- Environment: desk, light, noise, clutter, sleep, device distraction.
- Available feedback: teacher, practice tests, mentor, spaced repetition data.
- Constraints: work, family, health, learning difference, accommodations.

## Feng Shui Translation

| Feng shui concept | Learning translation |
| --- | --- |
| Qi flow | attention flow and study sequence |
| Ming tang | clear workspace and mental opening before hard work |
| Backing | curriculum, teacher, feedback, notes, sleep, stable desk |
| Sha qi | noise, deadlines, clutter, phone pings, fear, harsh self-talk |
| Leakage | multitasking, no review, scattered notes, weak recall |
| Timing | study, practice, review, rest, test cycle |

## Five-Phase Learning Lens

| Phase | Study role | Excess risk | Adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | curiosity, syllabus, growth plan | too many resources | metal pruning |
| Fire | motivation, performance, teaching others | burnout, cramming | water cooling |
| Earth | repetition, memory, routine | dullness | wood variation |
| Metal | testing, precision, correction | perfectionism | fire confidence |
| Water | deep reading, research, rest | avoidance | earth schedule |

## Environment Supports

- Keep the main desk visually clear and supported.
- Put only the active subject in the strongest visible position.
- Use light that supports alertness without glare.
- Reduce direct door/corridor rush, noise, and notification leakage.
- Build a review rhythm: learn, practice, recall, rest.

## Output Pattern

1. **Learning reality**: subject, deadline, current method, constraints.
2. **Environment read**: desk, light, noise, clutter, device leakage.
3. **Five-phase study balance**: growth, motivation, repetition, testing, rest.
4. **Practical plan**: schedule, review method, environment adjustment.
5. **Boundaries**: recommend qualified support when learning, health, or accessibility needs exceed the scope.

## Forbidden Claims

Do not claim a direction, color, desk placement, or date guarantees exam success, admission, memory, or mastery.
