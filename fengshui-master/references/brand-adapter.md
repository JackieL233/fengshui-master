# Brand Adapter

Use this file when the user asks for feng shui or five-phase guidance on brand strategy, naming, logo, colors, tone, launch identity, campaign direction, product positioning, visual system, or audience perception.

## Core Rule

Use feng shui and wuxing as symbolic design constraints, not as substitutes for audience research, accessibility, trademark review, usability, or brand strategy.

## Brand Intake

Ask for:

- Audience and desired impression.
- Product or service category.
- Brand stage: new, refresh, launch, crisis, repositioning.
- Competitors and category expectations.
- Required colors, forbidden colors, accessibility needs.
- Legal/trademark constraints.
- Where the brand appears: app, storefront, packaging, pitch deck, social, signage.

## Five-Phase Brand Lens

| Phase | Brand signal | Works for | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | fresh, growing, natural, learning | education, wellness-adjacent, startups, sustainability | immature, scattered |
| Fire | visible, exciting, bold, charismatic | media, launch, entertainment, campaigns | hype, fatigue |
| Earth | stable, trustworthy, grounded, caring | real estate, food, operations, community | dull, heavy |
| Metal | precise, premium, minimal, disciplined | finance controls, law, engineering, security, luxury | cold, inaccessible |
| Water | deep, adaptive, mysterious, networked | research, consulting, software, mobility, finance information | vague, low trust |

## Yin-Yang Brand Balance

- Too yang: loud, overdesigned, exhausting, hype-heavy.
- Too yin: hidden, vague, low contrast, hard to remember.
- Balance by matching category, audience risk tolerance, and channel.

## Form and Flow

Read brand touchpoints like a site:

- Entry: first word, logo, headline, packaging front, storefront sign.
- Path: can the user understand, trust, and act without friction?
- Backing: proof, credentials, reviews, support, documentation.
- Leakage: inconsistent names, inaccessible colors, unclear CTA, broken promise.
- Sha: aggressive copy, confusing symbols, legal conflict, cultural misuse.

## Output Pattern

1. **Brand reality**: audience, category, channels, constraints.
2. **Symbolic read**: phase and yin-yang balance.
3. **Risks**: accessibility, trust, confusion, overheat, under-visibility.
4. **Adjustments**: color, shape, wording, layout, launch timing, proof.
5. **Boundaries**: no replacement for research, testing, or legal review.

## Forbidden Claims

Do not claim a color, name, logo, or launch date guarantees fame, revenue, virality, or luck.
