# Relationship Adapter

Use this file when the user asks for feng shui or five-phase guidance on romantic relationships, family harmony, friendships, team relationships, shared living, conflict, communication flow, privacy, or relationship timing.

## Core Rule

Use feng shui as a symbolic and environmental support lens for communication, privacy, balance, and stress reduction. Do not predict marriage, divorce, pregnancy, affairs, reconciliation, or another person's feelings. Do not advise staying in unsafe relationships.

## Relationship Intake

Ask for:

- Relationship type: romantic, family, roommate, friendship, team, client.
- Goal: reduce conflict, improve communication, support privacy, make shared space calmer, decide timing, or understand pattern.
- Safety concerns or coercion.
- Shared-space context: bedroom, living room, dining area, workspace, noise, clutter, privacy.
- Communication constraints and boundaries.
- What can be changed without violating consent.

## Feng Shui Translation

| Feng shui concept | Relationship translation |
| --- | --- |
| Yin-yang balance | privacy and closeness, rest and action, listening and speaking |
| Pairing | symmetry, mutual recognition, shared space for two |
| Backing | trust, safety, family support, emotional reliability |
| Ming tang | room for conversation before pressure |
| Sha qi | sharp words, sharp corners, noise, surveillance, crowding, coercion |
| Leakage | unresolved resentment, poor boundaries, lack of follow-up |

## Five-Phase Relationship Lens

| Phase | Relationship expression | Excess risk | Support |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | growth, future plans, repair attempts | pressure to change | metal boundaries |
| Fire | warmth, attraction, joy, expression | drama, escalation | water listening |
| Earth | care, stability, shared routines | over-caretaking, stuckness | wood movement |
| Metal | boundaries, fairness, clarity | coldness, criticism | fire warmth |
| Water | empathy, depth, reflection | avoidance, secrecy | earth reliability |

## Space Supports

- Bedroom: reduce work, conflict, mirrors facing bed, and harsh lighting.
- Living area: create seating that allows conversation without confrontation.
- Dining area: support shared rituals if culturally and practically appropriate.
- Shared workspace: define boundaries and storage to reduce daily friction.
- Conflict zones: remove sharp, noisy, cluttered, or overheated triggers where possible.

## Output Pattern

1. **Relationship reality**: type, goal, safety, consent, constraints.
2. **Environment read**: privacy, seating, bedroom, noise, clutter, shared flow.
3. **Symbolic layer**: yin-yang, phases, backing, leakage, sha.
4. **Adjustments**: low-risk changes to space, rhythm, communication, boundaries.
5. **Limits**: no prediction of feelings or outcomes; recommend professional or safety support when needed.

## Forbidden Claims

Do not guarantee romance, marriage, pregnancy, reconciliation, loyalty, divorce prevention, or another person's behavior. If abuse, coercion, stalking, or danger appears, prioritize safety and qualified support.
