# Analysis Templates

Use these templates to structure user-facing feng shui readings. Keep the tone practical, transparent, and culturally grounded.

## Universal Intake

Ask only for what is needed:

- Purpose: buying, renting, improving, renovating, choosing layout, studying theory.
- Space type: home, apartment, office, shop, restaurant, land, room, desk, bed.
- Visual evidence: floor plan, photos, site map, surrounding roads/water/buildings.
- Direction data: facing, sitting, door, bed head, desk facing, stove, measured in degrees if possible.
- Time data: construction year, move-in year, major renovation year, intended move date if timing matters.
- People: number of occupants, main users, birth years only if personal-direction analysis is requested.
- Constraints: budget, rental restrictions, accessibility, pets/children, safety, style preferences.

## Residential Reading

Workflow:

1. Start outside: approach, road/water, noise, neighboring forms, entrance visibility.
2. Identify facing, sitting, and main qi mouth if possible.
3. Review entrance and ming tang.
4. Check plan regularity, center, missing/protruding sectors.
5. Review key rooms: bedroom, kitchen, bathroom, living/dining, study.
6. Check bed, stove, desk, sofa positions.
7. Add compass/personal/timing method only if data supports it.
8. Give prioritized remedies.

Suggested output:

- **Overall pattern**: 2-4 sentences.
- **Strengths**: what already supports qi.
- **Risks or frictions**: practical and symbolic.
- **Priority fixes**: top 3-7 actions.
- **Optional deeper reading**: what data is needed.

## Floor Plan Review

Checklist:

- Mark north and main entrance.
- Determine whether the plan is regular, missing corners, or heavily protruding.
- Locate center.
- Trace entry path and main circulation.
- Identify door-door, door-window, stove-sink, bed-door, desk-door relationships.
- Note bathrooms, kitchen, stairs, storage, and heavy fixtures.
- Confirm whether compass bagua or door-aligned bagua is used.

If no compass direction is available:

- Do a form and circulation reading.
- Do not assign compass sectors.
- Offer a clear list of readings the user can take.

## Bedroom

Priorities:

- Bed has a solid headboard or stable wall behind it.
- Bed is not directly in line with the door when avoidable.
- Sleeper can see the door without being hit by direct flow.
- Avoid heavy beams, shelves, sharp corners, mirrors reflecting the bed, or electronics dominating sleep.
- Balance both sides of the bed when shared.
- Keep lighting soft and air clean.

Common remedies:

- Move bed to command position.
- Add headboard, rug, curtains, bedside balance, warm low light.
- Screen direct door flow.
- Remove clutter under bed if it affects rest or air.
- Cover or relocate mirror if it disturbs sleep.

## Desk and Office

Priorities:

- Person has back support and can see the entrance.
- Avoid sitting with back directly to a door, corridor, or large window without support.
- Avoid direct collision with door/corridor flow.
- Keep the work surface clear, lit, and ergonomically usable.
- Use direction and personal gua only after basic command position is solved.

Business-office layers:

- Reception and entrance clarity.
- Leadership office stability.
- Team circulation and collaboration zones.
- Meeting-room authority and fairness.
- Finance/cash/security placement if relevant.

## Retail or Restaurant

Focus on qi as customer flow:

- Visibility from street or approach.
- Clear threshold and signage.
- Inviting but not chaotic entrance.
- Circulation that draws people inward and lets them pause.
- Cashier/order point visible but not blocking flow.
- Avoid dead corners, cluttered windows, harsh lighting, or confusing paths.
- Kitchen, storage, restroom, and staff flow must support operations.

Recommendations should include practical merchandising or operations where relevant.

## Site Selection

Assess:

- Terrain support and drainage.
- Road approach and traffic speed.
- Water presence and direction of movement if visible.
- Neighboring forms and pressure.
- Sunlight, wind, noise, pollution, access, and legal constraints.
- Shape of land parcel and buildable area.
- Entrance potential and open front.

Avoid:

- Making investment promises.
- Ignoring zoning, geotechnical, flood, fire, or environmental risks.

## Renovation and Remedies

Rank remedies:

1. Safety/code/repair.
2. Cleaning, decluttering, lighting, ventilation.
3. Furniture relocation.
4. Screens, curtains, plants, rugs, art, materials, color.
5. Minor construction.
6. Major renovation only with clear reason.

For each remedy, explain:

- What issue it addresses.
- Which framework supports it.
- Whether it is low/medium/high effort.
- What trade-off it creates.

## Output Rubric

A good answer:

- Names the method used.
- States assumptions.
- Separates observation from interpretation.
- Gives actionable priorities.
- Avoids fear-based language.
- Avoids guaranteed claims.
- Respects cultural origin and regional variation.
- Adapts to constraints and modern safety.
