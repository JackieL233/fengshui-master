# Remedies and Adjustments Reference

Use this file when the user asks how to improve, cure, adjust, activate, neutralize, or harmonize a space. Prefer practical, reversible, and safe changes.

## Remedy Principles

Remedies should solve an observed issue. Do not prescribe symbolic objects by default.

Good remedies are:

- Safe.
- Reversible.
- Maintained easily.
- Matched to the room's function.
- Explained through both practical and traditional reasoning.
- Scaled to the severity of the problem.

A remedy should answer:

- What condition is being addressed?
- Which framework is being used?
- What is the least disruptive change?
- What trade-off or maintenance burden appears?

## Remedy Priority Ladder

1. **Repair**: fix leaks, broken doors, poor lighting, mold, bad ventilation, unsafe wiring.
2. **Clear**: remove obstruction, trash, clutter, dead plants, blocked exits.
3. **Stabilize**: add backing, headboard, curtains, rugs, storage, acoustic control.
4. **Redirect**: screen, plant, partition, angle furniture, soften direct paths.
5. **Balance**: use color, material, shape, lighting, yin-yang, five phases.
6. **Activate**: add use, movement, light, sound, or attention where appropriate.
7. **Renovate**: move doors, walls, kitchen, bathroom, stairs only when justified.

| Desk back to door | weak support | interruptions, stress | rotate desk, add mirror only if comfortable, place solid backing |
| Beam over bed or seat | pressure | visual compression | move furniture, soften beam visually, canopy only if safe |

| Mirror faces bed | restless reflection | sleep disturbance | move, cover, angle, reduce glare |
| Stove and sink clash | fire-water tension | awkward workflow | improve spacing, add prep zone, use wood/earth bridge if symbolic |
| Toilet near center | draining center | odor, humidity, blocked circulation | ventilation, door closed, repair leaks, clean, stabilize center |

## Five-Phase Adjustments

Use the five phases as a light-touch vocabulary.

| Need | Consider | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Support wood | plants, vertical forms, green/blue-green, natural fibers | dead plants, overgrown clutter |
| Support fire | warm light, red accents, triangular forms, visibility | overheating, glare, aggressive red |
| Support earth | ceramics, stone, square forms, stable furniture, yellow/ochre | heaviness, dust, blocked center |
| Support metal | round forms, white/gray, metal objects, clarity, organization | coldness, sharpness, sterile feeling |
| Support water | dark blue/black, curved forms, reflective surface, gentle flow | leaks, unsafe water, excessive mirrors |

Control or soften:

- Too much wood: add metal clarity or fire transformation.
- Too much fire: add earth grounding or water cooling if safe.
- Too much earth: add wood movement or metal clarity.
- Too much metal: add fire warmth or water flow.
- Too much water: add earth containment or wood use.

## Object-Specific Guidance

### Mirrors

Use for light, expansion, visibility, and redirection. Avoid where they disturb sleep, create glare, reflect clutter, face a main door harshly, or compromise privacy.

### Plants

Use healthy plants for vitality and softening edges. Avoid thorny, dead, dusty, oversized, or badly placed plants.

### Water Features

Use only when maintenance, noise, child/pet safety, electrical safety, and placement make sense. Never add water solely because "wealth" is requested.

### Crystals, Coins, Gourds, Wind Chimes

Treat as lineage-specific or symbolic objects. If suggested, explain their symbolic purpose and offer non-object alternatives.

### Color

Use color as a minor adjustment. Do not repaint major surfaces unless the practical and aesthetic result also works.

## What Not to Recommend

- Blocking exits or windows.
- Hanging heavy objects over beds or seats.
- Adding water near electricity without protection.
- Encouraging candles in unsafe locations.
- Creating accessibility barriers.
- Suggesting expensive renovation for a minor symbolic issue.
- Using fear to force a cure.
