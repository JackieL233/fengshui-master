# Legal-Adjacent Adapter

Use this file when the user asks to apply feng shui, five phases, timing, or symbolic reasoning to contracts, disputes, negotiations with legal stakes, compliance, leases, employment terms, inheritance, court-adjacent decisions, or risk review.

## Core Rule

This is not legal advice. Use feng shui only as a symbolic decision-support and preparation lens. Legal rights, obligations, deadlines, evidence, jurisdiction, contracts, filings, and counsel must take priority.

## Legal-Adjacent Intake

Ask for:

- Decision type: contract review, negotiation, dispute, lease, employment term, compliance, filing, risk planning.
- Jurisdiction or governing context if the user volunteers it.
- Hard deadlines and required procedures.
- Stakeholders and incentives.
- Documents or clauses the user can summarize.
- Whether a qualified lawyer, advisor, or authority is already involved.

Do not ask for privileged, confidential, or sensitive details beyond what is necessary.

| Feng shui concept | Legal-adjacent translation |
| --- | --- |
| Backing | documents, evidence, counsel, witnesses, clear records |
| Ming tang | room to negotiate before deadline pressure |
| Sha qi | threats, vague terms, hidden liability, urgency, hostile communication |
| Leakage | missed deadlines, undocumented promises, ambiguous scope, fees |
| Metal | rules, precision, clauses, boundaries, documentation |
| Water | information flow, listening, alternative paths |

## Five-Phase Legal Lens

| Phase | Legal-adjacent role | Risk | Adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | options and future relationship | vague expansion | metal specificity |
| Fire | urgency, public pressure, emotion | escalation | water cooling |
| Earth | stability, settlement, reliability | stuck compromise | wood alternatives |
| Metal | contract terms, evidence, rules | rigidity | water context |
| Water | negotiation channels, information | ambiguity | earth recordkeeping |

## Output Pattern

1. **Legal reality first**: deadlines, documents, jurisdiction, counsel, required procedures.
2. **Risk map**: hidden liability, ambiguity, urgency, evidence gaps, communication leakage.
3. **Symbolic layer**: support, sha, leakage, metal clarity, water information.
4. **Preparation actions**: organize documents, list questions for counsel, clarify goals, avoid rushed commitments.
5. **Boundary**: consult qualified legal help; do not rely on feng shui for rights or obligations.

## Forbidden Claims

Do not interpret law, draft binding legal advice, predict court outcomes, tell users to ignore deadlines, or recommend signing/refusing solely by feng shui symbolism.
