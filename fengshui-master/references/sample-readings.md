## Apartment Without Compass

User asks: "Can you review my apartment floor plan? I do not know north."

Pattern:

```text
I can do a form-school and practical flow reading without north. I will not assign compass sectors yet.

Inputs and assumptions:
- Plan visible, but no north arrow.
- Main door appears at the lower left of the plan.

Method:
- Form school, entrance flow, bedroom command position, kitchen/bathroom relationship, light/air checks.

Findings:
- The entrance has a short pause zone, which is better than a door opening straight into the bed or kitchen.
- The main corridor runs quickly toward the rear window; traditionally this can read as qi passing through too fast, and practically it may feel like the home has no settling point.

Priority actions:
1. Add a small console, rug, or lighting focus near the entry to create a landing point.
2. Keep the corridor clear but visually softened.
3. Send a north reading if you want compass bagua or flying-star work.
```

## Desk Placement With Bearing

User asks: "My desk faces 92 degrees and the door is behind me."

Pattern:

```text
92 degrees is near the mao / 卯 mountain, an east-sector reading. That bearing alone is not enough to judge auspiciousness.

The stronger issue is form: your back is to the door, which is usually weaker than a less favorable personal direction with good support.

Priority:
1. Rotate the desk so you can see the door if possible.
2. If rotation is impossible, add solid backing and a small sightline aid.
3. If you want eight mansions, provide birth year and the traditional sex convention you want to use.
```

## Retail Entrance Comparison

User asks: "Which storefront is better?"

Pattern:

```text
I would score this as customer-flow feng shui first, not formula feng shui.

Option A:
- Better visibility and broader ming tang.
- Door is easy to find.
- Interior path has a natural first pause.

Option B:
- Cheaper rent, but the entrance is hidden and the sidewalk flow moves past it quickly.
- Window glare weakens product visibility.

Recommendation:
Option A is stronger unless rent changes the business model. It has fewer entrance problems and needs fewer remedies.
```

## Period 9 Flying-Star Intake

User asks: "My house was built in 2026. Is it good in Period 9?"

Pattern:

```text
2026 falls in the common Period 9 range, 2024-2043. That does not make the house automatically good.

For xuan kong I still need:
- Facing direction in degrees.
- Whether 2026 is completion, move-in, or major renovation.
- Floor plan with north.
- Main door, bedroom, kitchen, and water locations.

Without those, I can only explain Period 9 themes and do a form-school review.
```

## Yin House Conceptual Review

User asks: "Is this cemetery plot good?"

Pattern:

```text
I can give a conservative yin-house form review, but I cannot make deterministic claims about descendants or replace local legal/cemetery guidance.

Visible priorities:
- Rear support appears stable.
- Front is open but not overly exposed.
- Drainage and soil stability need confirmation.

Concerns:
- A straight path appears to point at the plot. Traditionally this can be read as harsh qi; practically it may mean foot traffic and maintenance disturbance.

Low-impact next steps:
- Confirm drainage and cemetery rules.
- Visit after rain.
- Document the compass facing if advanced lineage analysis is wanted.
```
