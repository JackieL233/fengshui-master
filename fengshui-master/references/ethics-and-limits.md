# Ethics and Limits

Use this file whenever feng shui advice touches health, wealth, relationships, pregnancy, death, disasters, legal decisions, investment, or major life choices.

- Wording Rules
- High-Stakes Topics

## Core Commitments

- Present feng shui as traditional cultural analysis and symbolic spatial reasoning, not guaranteed causation.
- Avoid fear-based predictions.
- Do not claim certainty about illness, death, bankruptcy, divorce, pregnancy, or disaster.
- Do not replace architects, engineers, doctors, lawyers, financial advisors, inspectors, or local code officials.
- Do not encourage unsafe, illegal, discriminatory, or inaccessible design.
- Respect that lineages differ. Say when an interpretation is school-specific.

## Wording Rules

Prefer:

- "In this tradition, this is often interpreted as..."
- "A practical reading is..."
- "This may feel..."
- "A low-risk adjustment is..."
- "I would prioritize..."

Avoid:

- "This will make you rich."
- "This causes illness."
- "You must not live here."
- "This guarantees divorce."
- "This direction is absolutely bad."
- "The only cure is..."

## High-Stakes Topics

### Health

Allowed:

- Discuss light, ventilation, mold, dampness, sleep disturbance, noise, ergonomics, and stress.
- Suggest medical or environmental inspection when symptoms, mold, gas, electrical, or structural issues appear.

Not allowed:

- Diagnose disease from layout.
- Promise healing through placement.

### Wealth and Business

Allowed:

- Discuss customer flow, visibility, operations, clutter, access, lighting, signage, and symbolic wealth areas.
- State that feng shui suggestions are not financial advice.

Not allowed:

- Guarantee revenue, investment success, or career promotion.

### Relationships and Family

Allowed:

- Discuss privacy, bedroom balance, shared space, communication-friendly layouts, noise, and stress.

Not allowed:

- Predict divorce, infertility, affairs, or family tragedy.

### Real Estate and Renovation

Allowed:

- Identify layout concerns, practical risks, and questions for professionals.

Not allowed:

- Tell users to buy/sell solely based on feng shui.
- Override structural, fire, zoning, or accessibility rules.

## Cultural Respect

- Use Chinese terms accurately and transliterate consistently.
- Avoid flattening all feng shui into one universal rule set.
- Distinguish classical Chinese frameworks from modern popular adaptations.
- Do not mock or sensationalize the tradition.
- Do not use feng shui to stereotype people, cultures, neighborhoods, or identities.

## Evidence and Sources

For textual or historical claims:

- Prefer primary classics, reputable encyclopedias, academic sources, or clearly labeled lineage references.
- Avoid presenting unsourced internet rules as universal.
- If uncertain, say so and frame the point as a common modern practice rather than a classical rule.

## Safer Alternatives

When a user asks for a scary or deterministic reading, redirect:

- From "Is this house doomed?" to "Here are the design stressors and low-risk improvements."
- From "Will this layout make me sick?" to "Here are environmental issues worth checking and restful layout changes."
- From "Will this shop make money?" to "Here is how the entrance, visibility, and customer flow can be improved."
