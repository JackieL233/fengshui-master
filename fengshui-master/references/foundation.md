## Principles

Feng shui, literally "wind-water", is a Chinese spatial tradition concerned with how qi is gathered, moved, protected, and dispersed through landforms, buildings, directions, timing, and human use. Treat it as a traditional cultural system, not as a deterministic science.

Core reading layers:

- **Qi**: vitality, movement, containment, circulation, and dispersal.
- **Yin-yang**: relative balance of stillness/activity, shade/light, enclosure/openness, quiet/motion.
- **Five phases**: wood, fire, earth, metal, water as relationships of generation and control.
- **Image and form**: mountains, water, roads, building shapes, door paths, corners, beams, and proportions.
- **Direction and time**: compass sectors, trigrams, 24 mountains, occupant or building timing methods.
- **Human fit**: purpose, comfort, safety, cultural expectations, and practical constraints.

## Yin-Yang

Yin and yang are relational qualities, not fixed moral labels.

| Yin | Yang |
| --- | --- |
| still, inward, shaded, cool, enclosed, quiet | active, outward, bright, warm, open, loud |

Use yin-yang to identify imbalance:

- Bedrooms usually benefit from more yin: quiet, stable, soft light, lower stimulation.
- Entrances and work areas need enough yang: visibility, access, movement, activity.
- Too much yin can read as damp, stagnant, isolated, dark, or underused.
- Too much yang can read as exposed, noisy, restless, overheated, or hard to settle.

## Five Phases

The five phases are a relational cycle, often used for symbolic diagnosis and adjustment.

| Phase | Common correspondences | Generates | Controls |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | growth, verticality, plants, green/blue-green, east/southeast | Fire | Earth |
| Fire | brightness, heat, red/purple, triangular forms, south | Earth | Metal |
| Earth | stability, ceramics, stone, square forms, center/northeast/southwest | Metal | Water |
| Metal | clarity, round forms, white/gray/metallic, west/northwest | Water | Wood |
| Water | flow, reflection, black/dark blue, wavy forms, north | Wood | Fire |

Cycle use:

- **Generating cycle**: wood -> fire -> earth -> metal -> water -> wood.
- **Controlling cycle**: wood controls earth, earth controls water, water controls fire, fire controls metal, metal controls wood.
- Apply gently. A small material, color, shape, or use-pattern change is often enough in symbolic advice.

## Bagua

The eight trigrams are a symbolic map used across many Chinese traditions.

| Trigram | Pinyin | Image | Family role | Common direction in later-heaven bagua | Phase |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 乾 | qian | heaven | father | northwest | metal |
| 坤 | kun | earth | mother | southwest | earth |
| 震 | zhen | thunder | eldest son | east | wood |
| 巽 | xun | wind/wood | eldest daughter | southeast | wood |
| 坎 | kan | water | middle son | north | water |
| 离 | li | fire | middle daughter | south | fire |
| 艮 | gen | mountain | youngest son | northeast | earth |
| 兑 | dui | lake/marsh | youngest daughter | west | metal |

Use bagua carefully:

- **Compass bagua** uses actual directions.
- **Door-aligned or symbolic bagua** overlays the plan from the main entrance in some modern schools.
- Do not mix these without saying which one is being applied.

## Nine Palace Grid

Use the nine palace grid when discussing xuan kong flying stars, annual stars, or compass-sector floor-plan overlays.

| Palace | Later-heaven direction | Trigram | Phase |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | north | kan | water |
| 2 | southwest | kun | earth |
| 3 | east | zhen | wood |
| 4 | southeast | xun | wood |
| 5 | center | center | earth |
| 6 | northwest | qian | metal |
| 7 | west | dui | metal |
| 8 | northeast | gen | earth |
| 9 | south | li | fire |

Grid use:

- Align the grid to a reliable north arrow for compass-based work.
- Do not use the same grid as a door-aligned bagua without saying the method changed.
- For flying stars, the grid is only a container; chart construction also needs period and facing/sitting assumptions.

## Compass Inputs

Clarify what the bearing represents:

- **Facing direction**: the active, open, or yang side of a building or unit.
- **Sitting direction**: opposite the facing direction.
- **Main door direction**: the door's facing or the direction one faces when exiting; traditions vary, so ask or state convention.
- **Bed head direction**: direction toward which the headboard points.
- **Desk facing direction**: direction the seated person faces while working.
- **Stove mouth direction**: tradition-specific; ask before applying.

Compass hygiene:

- Ask whether the reading is magnetic or true north if precision matters.
- Avoid readings near large metal, appliances, vehicles, elevators, or reinforced concrete.
- Take repeated readings and average when possible.

## Practical Interpretation Rules

- Begin with visible form and usability before symbolic correction.
- Use five-phase remedies lightly: material, color, shape, light, water, plant, sound, or use-pattern.
- Avoid adding water, mirrors, plants, or bright colors as universal cures. They can be wrong by room, direction, safety, maintenance, or user preference.
- Prefer reversible interventions: declutter, lighting, circulation, furniture placement, repair, cleaning, privacy, acoustic control.
