## Scope and Boundaries

Yin house feng shui is specialized and lineage-sensitive. This skill can provide conceptual structure, intake questions, and conservative form analysis. It should not claim to replace an experienced local practitioner, surveyor, cemetery authority, engineer, legal advisor, or family ritual specialist.

Avoid exact claims about descendants' wealth, illness, fertility, or death.

## Yin House vs Yang House

| Yang house | Yin house |
| --- | --- |
| living spaces, daily use, human comfort | burial or ancestral resting place |
| entry, circulation, light, air, rooms | landform, burial orientation, water, soil, protection |
| practical remedies often possible | interventions may be legally, ritually, or emotionally sensitive |
| occupant preferences matter directly | family consensus, law, cemetery rules, ritual custom matter strongly |

Do not casually transfer house rules to burial sites.

## Core Intake

Ask:

- Is this an existing grave, new cemetery plot, ancestral tomb, or academic question?
- What country/region and cemetery rules apply?
- Are there photos, map, contour, or site plan?
- Where are higher land, lower land, roads, water, paths, neighboring graves, trees, walls, and buildings?
- What is the intended facing direction if known?
- Are there legal, religious, family, or maintenance constraints?
- Is any physical change actually allowed?

## Form Priorities

Conservative form reading:

- Stable rear support.
- Gentle embrace from sides.
- Open but not exposed front.
- Clean drainage and dry stable soil.
- No harsh road, path, water, pole, wall, or sharp structure directly striking the site.
- Avoid erosion, flooding, landslide risk, root damage, vandalism, or inaccessible maintenance.
- Respect cemetery order and neighboring graves.

Use "mountain" and "water" flexibly but do not ignore actual geology, drainage, and law.

## Directional Work

Directional analysis may involve:

- Luopan reading.
- 24 mountains.
- San he water and branch relationships.
- San yuan period and lineage formulas.
-分金 and local lineage rules.

If exact direction work is requested, say what data is missing and avoid inventing formulas.

## Ethics and Safety

Yin house topics often involve grief, family conflict, religion, and local law.

Rules:

- Use calm, respectful language.
- Do not create fear about ancestors harming descendants.
- Do not recommend illegal exhumation, relocation, or alteration.
- Encourage family consensus and cemetery/legal consultation.
- Treat photos and locations as sensitive personal data.

## Output Pattern

1. State the limits and respect context.
2. Identify visible form strengths.
3. Identify practical risks: drainage, erosion, access, legal limits, maintenance.
4. Identify traditional concerns as symbolic or lineage-dependent.
5. Recommend low-impact actions: cleaning, maintenance, drainage inquiry, respectful planting, documentation.
6. List data needed for advanced directional reading.
