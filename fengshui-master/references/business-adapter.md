# Business Adapter

Use this file when the user asks for feng shui guidance on business strategy, operations, revenue, customer flow, store performance, company growth, partnerships, hiring, product-market fit, fundraising, or organizational decisions.

## Core Rule

Use feng shui as a symbolic and operational lens for flow, support, leakage, timing, and balance. Do not replace business fundamentals, accounting, law, tax, customer research, safety, or management judgment.

## Business Intake

Ask for:

- Business model and stage.
- Main goal: revenue, retention, launch, hiring, cash flow, customer experience, partnership, or operations.
- Customer path or sales funnel.
- Constraints: budget, runway, staffing, regulation, lease, supply chain, technology.
- Current bottleneck or leakage.
- Physical site, website, product flow, or process map if relevant.

## Feng Shui Translation

| Feng shui concept | Business translation |
| --- | --- |
| Ming tang | market opening, demand space, first customer impression |
| Backing | capital, team, supplier reliability, data, advisors, brand trust |
| Qi flow | customer journey, lead flow, information flow, operational handoff |
| Sha qi | hostile deadlines, legal risk, confusing offer, bad reviews, bottlenecks |
| Leakage | churn, fees, waste, discounting, poor follow-up, cash burn |
| Stagnation | stale offer, slow inventory, no feedback loop, unused assets |

## Five-Phase Business Lens

| Phase | Business role | Excess risk | Balancing move |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wood | growth pipeline, product roadmap, hiring | overexpansion | metal prioritization |
| Fire | marketing, launch, public attention | hype, burnout | earth delivery discipline |
| Earth | retention, trust, operations | bureaucracy, inertia | wood experimentation |
| Metal | process, compliance, margin control | rigidity, overcutting | water customer discovery |
| Water | cash flow, research, networks | ambiguity, drift | earth metrics and cadence |

## Decision Pattern

1. **Business reality**: customer, margin, cash, team, constraints.
2. **Flow diagnosis**: acquisition, conversion, fulfillment, retention.
3. **Feng shui layer**: support, leakage, sha, timing, phase balance.
4. **Action ladder**: clarify offer, remove friction, add support, set cadence.
5. **Boundaries**: no guaranteed revenue, valuation, funding, or market outcome.

## Useful Low-Risk Adjustments

- Clarify the entrance: headline, signage, landing page, first offer.
- Create a bright hall: give customers enough space to understand the offer before pressure.
- Reduce leakage: follow-up system, pricing discipline, cash review, churn review.
- Add backing: testimonials, guarantees that are legally safe, documentation, advisor review.
- Use timing as cadence: launch, review, repair, and rest cycles.

## Forbidden Claims

Do not say feng shui guarantees sales, investment, funding, employee loyalty, partnership success, or market timing.
