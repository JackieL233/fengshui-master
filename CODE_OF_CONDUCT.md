# Code of Conduct

## Respectful Collaboration

FengShui Master is an open-source project about traditional Chinese feng shui, wuxing, spatial analysis, and broad symbolic decision support. Contributors should treat traditional Chinese culture, regional practices, lineages, and modern adaptations with care.

We welcome:

- Good-faith corrections to terminology, translation, pinyin, school boundaries, and source posture.
- Practical safety improvements.
- Clear examples that separate observation, interpretation, and recommendation.
- Respectful disagreement about lineage-specific methods when the disagreement is labeled and sourced.

We do not welcome:

- Harassment, insults, identity-based attacks, or dismissive treatment of cultural traditions.
- Fear-based claims about curses, doom, illness, death, pregnancy, marriage, disaster, or fate.
- No fear-based claims packaged as certain feng shui authority.
- Medical, legal, financial, engineering, tax, architectural, psychological, or safety advice presented as guaranteed by feng shui.
- Plagiarized or long copyrighted passages.
- Attempts to pressure maintainers into endorsing one school as universally correct without scope or evidence.

## Contribution Tone

Use clear, sourced, and bounded language. Prefer:

- "In this school, this pattern is commonly read as..."
- "This is a symbolic interpretation, not a guaranteed outcome."
- "A safer practical adjustment is..."

Avoid:

- "This always causes..."
- "This person/home/business is doomed."
- "This guarantees wealth, illness, marriage, loss, or profit."

## Reporting

If you see conduct that harms collaboration or safety, open a GitHub issue when appropriate, or contact the repository owner through GitHub. Include the relevant comment, issue, pull request, or file path, and explain the impact.

Maintainers may edit, close, or remove contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, especially when they introduce unsafe claims, disrespect traditional Chinese culture, or blur professional advice boundaries.
